Unni Wikan's Concept of Resonance

→ Experience Resonance

Beyond Words

Resonance requires engaging with both verbal and non-verbal communication to achieve deeper understanding. It involves:

  • Attending to gestures, emotions, and silences
  • Recognizing that 90% of communication is non-verbal
  • Moving past surface-level narratives to grasp underlying meanings

The Power of Resonance

Shared Human Experience

Resonance evokes and builds upon shared human experiences, allowing people to connect across cultural differences. This approach:

  • Deexoticizes people's compelling concerns
  • Foregrounds immediate and mundane experiences rather than the spectacular
  • Creates possibilities for enhanced human solidarity

Methodological Approach

Wikan advocates starting with people's compelling concerns as they emerge in everyday life. This requires:

  • Using one's own life experience to understand others
  • Engaging with another's world through feeling-thought
  • Maintaining a willingness to connect with different lives and ideas

Attending to Compelling Concerns

Person-Centered Understanding

Wikan's approach emphasizes understanding people on their own terms by:

  • Focusing on immediate and practical concerns
  • Recognizing shared human experiences across cultures
  • Moving beyond cultural stereotypes and exoticization

Practical Application

The concept has been applied across diverse settings, from studying:

  • Poverty in Cairo
  • Childbirth in Bhutan
  • Honor killings in Scandinavia
  • Transvestites in Oman
